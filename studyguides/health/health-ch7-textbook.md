## Drugs Used as Medicine

A **drug** is a substance that changes how the mind or body works. A **medicine** is a drug used to prevent, treat, or cure a health condition.

Your parents or guardian may buy medicine without a doctor's order. **Over-the-counter (OTC) drugs** are medicines that can be bought without a doctor's order. A medicine that can be obtained only with a doctor's order is a **prescription drug**.

There are different kinds of medicines. *Antibiotics* help your body fight off some infections. *Vaccines* keep you from getting some illnesses. Other medicines reduce pain or swelling.

Many drugs have brand names. Companies think the brand names will make people want to buy their drugs. Some drugs are generic drugs. They have no brand names but have the same main ingredients as brand-name drugs. They are often cheaper than drugs with brand names. A generic drug and a brand-name drug may do the same thing.

## Safety Rules for Medicine

Medicines can help your health. But they can harm you if they aren't used safely. Here are some important safety rules for using medicines.

- **Take medicine only from your parents or guardian** or another adult they approve of. Your parent or guardian may give permission to someone else to give you medicine if he or she is unable to. Your parent or guardian will tell you who may give you medicine.

- **Follow the instructions** that come with your medicine. Take the right dose. A *dose* is the amount of medicine you should take. Your doctor may tell you the dose. An OTC package may list a dose. The label also may tell you how to take the medicine. You may need to take it with food. You may need to drink lots of water with the medicine.

- **Tell your doctor and pharmacist** about any medicines you take. Sometimes drugs can change the action of other drugs when taken together. These changes can make the drugs not work properly. They also can harm you.

- **Take only your own medicines**, not someone else's. Other people's medicines may be too strong for you. They may not be the right medicine for you.

> [SIDEBAR: "Do You Know?"] Companies that sell OTC medicines must label them with safe doses. If the safe dose is different for children, they must say so on the label.

## Drug Misuse and Abuse

You have an ear infection. A doctor prescribed medicine for you. You are supposed to take two pills each day. You take four pills by mistake. This is drug misuse. **Drug misuse** is the accidental unsafe use of a medicine. If you misuse a drug, tell your parents or guardian right away. Your parent or guardian can decide what to do. He or she may call a doctor.

Some people use legal drugs in unsafe ways on purpose. They like how the drugs make them feel. Other people use illegal drugs. An **illegal drug** is a drug that is against the law to have, use, buy, or sell. The use of an illegal drug or the harmful use of a legal drug is called **drug abuse**. Abusing drugs can harm your mind and your body.

Drug abuse can lead to an addiction. An **addiction** is a strong desire to do something even though it is harmful. People who have a drug addiction are dependent on drugs. They depend on drugs for emotional or physical effects. Addiction can lead to serious health problems and even death.

### What Is Alcohol?

**Alcohol** is a depressant drug found in some drinks. A **depressant** is a drug that slows down the body. Alcohol slows down the brain and other parts of the nervous system. Even small amounts of alcohol affect the body.

### Alcohol and the Body

When a person drinks alcohol, it goes directly into the bloodstream from the stomach and small intestine. **Blood alcohol concentration (BAC)** is the amount of alcohol in a person’s blood. BAC depends on how much alcohol a person drinks, how fast the person drinks, and the person’s body size.

## Effects of Alcohol

**Intoxication** (in•TAHK•suh•KAY•shuhn) is the physical and mental changes produced by drinking alcohol. A person who is intoxicated is drunk. **Alcoholism** is a disease in which a person is dependent on alcohol.

### Short-term Effects

A **minor** is a person who is under the legal age. It is against the law for minors to use alcohol.

**Difficulty Thinking Clearly** Alcohol is a depressant that slows down the brain. A person who has been drinking has difficulty thinking clearly and making responsible decisions.

**Poorer Coordination and Slower Reaction Time** A person who has been drinking has poorer coordination. Reaction time is slower. A person may have slurred speech.

**Stronger Emotions** A person who has been drinking may have stronger emotions. The person may become angry or sad more quickly.

**Breaking Family Guidelines** Young people who drink often break family guidelines.

### Long-term Effects

A person who drinks alcohol over a long period of time may develop serious health problems.

**Liver Disease** The liver changes alcohol into substances the body can use or get rid of. Too much alcohol can damage the liver. Cirrhosis (suh•ROH•suhs) is a disease in which liver cells are damaged and replaced by scar tissue. The liver does not work well. Alcoholic hepatitis (heh•puh•TY•tuhs) is a swelling of the liver caused by drinking too much alcohol. Both diseases can cause death.

**Heart Disease and Cancer** Long-term alcohol use can cause heart disease. Alcohol use can also increase the risk of developing cancer of the mouth, throat, and stomach.

**Damage to Organs** Long-term alcohol use can cause the pancreas to become inflamed. It can cause stomach bleeding.

**Birth Defects** A pregnant woman who drinks alcohol can cause birth defects in her baby. Fetal alcohol syndrome is a group of problems caused by the effects of alcohol on an unborn baby.

**Social Problems** A person who abuses alcohol may have social problems. He or she may have difficulty getting along with family and friends and difficulty performing well at school or work.

## Reasons Not to Drink Alcohol

There are many reasons not to drink alcohol. Here are some of them.

**1. I want to obey laws.** It is against the law for minors to buy or use alcohol.

**2. I want to make responsible decisions.** Drinking alcohol affects the way people think. A person who drinks may make decisions he or she would not otherwise make.

**3. I want to keep my senses to function at their best.** Alcohol can cause blurred vision. It can slow reaction time.

**4. I want to keep from having accidents.** Drinking and driving is very dangerous. Many people are killed every year by people who drink and drive. Alcohol slows down reaction time. A person may not be able to react fast enough to prevent an accident.

**5. I want to have healthful relationships.** A person who drinks may have difficulty getting along with family and friends.

**6. I want to get along well with my family.** Many families have guidelines against drinking. A young person who drinks may break family guidelines.

**7. I want to keep from being depressed.** Alcohol is a depressant. A person who uses alcohol may become depressed.

**10. I want to not have alcoholism.** People who have a history of alcoholism in their families have a greater risk of becoming dependent on alcohol. The best way to prevent alcoholism is to stay alcohol free.

## Toxins in Tobacco

There are many harmful substances in tobacco and tobacco smoke.

**Nicotine** (NIK•uh•teen) is a stimulant drug found in tobacco. A stimulant is a drug that speeds up body functions. Nicotine speeds up the heartbeat and affects the brain. Nicotine is very addictive.

**Tar** is a thick, gummy substance found in tobacco smoke. Tar sticks to the linings of the airways. The airways make mucus to try to get rid of the tar. Tar can kill the cells that line the airways.

**Carbon Monoxide** (KAR•buhn muh•NAHK•sighd) is a poisonous gas found in tobacco smoke. Carbon monoxide takes the place of some of the oxygen in the blood. This makes it hard for the blood to carry oxygen to body cells.

**Smokeless Tobacco** is tobacco that is chewed or placed between the cheek and gums. High levels of nicotine enter the body through the gums when smokeless tobacco is used.

## Short-term Effects of Tobacco

**Addiction** Nicotine in tobacco is very addictive. A person who starts using tobacco may quickly become addicted.

**Breathing Problems** Carbon monoxide in tobacco smoke means the blood can’t carry as much oxygen. A person who smokes may be short of breath. It is harder to be physically active or participate in sports.

**Stains and Smells** Nicotine and tar in tobacco smoke stain the hands and teeth. Tobacco smoke causes bad breath and makes clothes and hair smell.

**Cost** Using tobacco is an expensive habit. Money spent on tobacco products can’t be spent on other things.

**Fires and Burns** Smoking increases the risk of fires and burns. Smoking is a leading cause of fire deaths in the United States.

# Page 19 (D19)

## Long-term Effects

Smokers are about one and a half times as likely to die early from disease as nonsmokers.

**Heart Disease** Carbon monoxide in tobacco smoke makes the heart work harder to get oxygen to body cells. Nicotine makes the heart beat faster. Both of these increase the risk of heart disease.

**Lung Disease** Smoking causes more than three-quarters of all lung cancer cases. Smokeless tobacco increases the risk of cancer of the mouth and throat. **Emphysema** (em•fuh•ZEE•muh) is a disease in which the alveoli in the lungs are destroyed. Smoking can keep a young person’s lungs from growing. Smokers have more sore throats and coughs.

**Stomach Ulcers** Stomach ulcers are open sores. Tobacco users are more likely to develop stomach ulcers.

**Other Effects** Smoking causes wrinkled skin and unhealthy gums. Teeth may fall out. The senses of taste and smell are dulled. Pregnant women who smoke are more likely to have babies with health problems.

**Breaking Laws** It is illegal for people under the age of 18 to buy tobacco products.

# Page 20 (D20)

## How to Quit Tobacco Use

Nicotine is addictive, so using tobacco is hard to stop. A person who quits using tobacco starts to reduce the risk of disease in just one day. There are programs to help a person stop using tobacco. The American Lung Association and the American Cancer Society have programs to help people quit. Hospitals and clinics can also help. Medicines can help a person deal with the physical effects of quitting.

**Tips to Quit Tobacco Use:**
- Set a date to quit.
- Throw away all tobacco products.
- Stay away from other tobacco users.
- Find activities to replace tobacco use.
- Ask family and friends for support.

---

## Secondhand Smoke

Exhaled smoke and smoke from the burning end of a cigarette, cigar, or pipe is called **secondhand smoke**. Secondhand smoke is a **carcinogen** (kar•SI•nuh•juhn). A carcinogen is something that can cause cancer.

About 3,000 nonsmokers die every year from lung cancer caused by secondhand smoke. Secondhand smoke can cause heart disease. Young people who breathe secondhand smoke have a higher risk of lung problems. They have asthma more often. They have more lung infections, such as pneumonia (new•MOHN•yuh) and bronchitis (brong•KIGH•tis).

# Page 25 (D25)

## How Drug Abuse Harms Health

You read in Lesson 1 that drug abuse is the use of an illegal drug or the harmful use of a legal drug. Drug abuse harms health in many different ways.

**Addiction** A person who abuses a drug can become dependent on the drug. When the person is dependent he or she is addicted. Addictions are very difficult to stop.

**Tolerance** Over time a person can build up a tolerance to a drug. **Tolerance** is a condition in which more of a drug is needed to get the same effect. This increases the risk of an overdose. An **overdose** is too large an amount of a medicine or drug. An overdose can cause serious illness or even death.

**Mental and Physical Problems** Drug abuse harms the mind and body. It makes it difficult to concentrate and do well in school and sports. Over time drug abuse can harm the mind and body permanently.

**Social Problems** A person who abuses drugs may lose the trust and respect of family and friends. Drugs can make it hard to meet new people and make new friends, too.

**Legal Problems** Many drugs are illegal. Buying, selling, or using these drugs can send a person to jail.

**Cost** Drugs are expensive. People who are addicted to drugs may steal money to buy drugs. They may commit other crimes for money, too.

**Withdrawal** People who have addictions and then stop using drugs may go through withdrawal. **Withdrawal** is the unpleasant reaction experienced when a drug is no longer taken. People going through withdrawal may shake or feel nauseous. They may see and hear things that aren't there. The symptoms of withdrawal can be very serious and can even cause death.

## Marijuana, Depressants, and Stimulants

Marijuana, stimulants, and depressants are drugs that people commonly abuse. **Marijuana** (mehr•uh•WAH•nuh) is a drug that is made from the cannabis plant. **Depressants** are drugs that slow down body functions. **Stimulants** are drugs that speed up body functions.

### Marijuana

Most people who abuse marijuana smoke it. Some eat it in food. Marijuana is illegal in most places.

People use marijuana because it makes them feel high. This is caused by a chemical in marijuana called THC. THC changes the way people think and feel. People can become dependent on marijuana.

People who abuse marijuana can have trouble thinking and remembering things. People who smoke marijuana may be more likely to get lung cancer. Marijuana also speeds up the heart. It increases appetite and slows down reaction time.

> [IMAGE: Close-up photo of a marijuana/cannabis plant.]
>
> *Marijuana comes from a plant called hemp or cannabis.*

### Depressants

Some depressants are legal if a doctor prescribes them. A doctor might prescribe tranquilizers (TRAN•kwuh•ly•zerz) to help relieve anxiety. Barbiturates (bar•BIH•chuh•ruhts) are like tranquilizers but they are even stronger. They make people sleepy.

These depressants slow reaction time. They reduce blood pressure and slow the heart. They also slow the thinking part of the brain. It is very dangerous to take them with alcohol and can even cause death.

Some people abuse depressants to help them relax. They become dependent and can't relax without the depressants.

Stimulants make a person feel more alert. When some stimulants wear off, a person can become very depressed. This can even lead to suicide. A person may keep taking the stimulant to avoid this feeling. He or she becomes dependent.

### Caffeine

Many people consume caffeine (ka•FEEN) every day. Caffeine is a legal stimulant found in coffee, tea, some sodas, and chocolate. Caffeine helps people feel alert. It can speed up the heart. It might irritate a person's stomach lining. You can become addicted to caffeine.

### Amphetamines

Amphetamines (am•FE•tuh•meenz) are drugs that increase alertness. Some amphetamines are legal with a prescription. Some diet pills contain amphetamines. Amphetamines also can help people with some kinds of sleep disorders or attention problems.

Both legal and illegal amphetamines can be addictive. These drugs speed up the heart and raise blood pressure. Abusing amphetamines can cause an irregular heart beat and brain damage.

### Cocaine

Cocaine (koh•KAYN) is an illegal stimulant made from leaves of the coca plant. Cocaine makes a person feel high, and then very depressed. People usually inhale it. Crack is a very strong form of cocaine that people smoke. A person can become addicted to either one after just one or two uses. Both can cause brain damage.

### Ecstasy

Ecstasy, or MDMA, is an illegal drug that is both a stimulant and a depressant. It can harm the heart and brain.

## Narcotics, Inhalants, and Steroids

Some drugs are neither stimulants nor depressants. These drugs include narcotics, inhalants, and steroids.

### Narcotics

**Narcotics** (nahr•KAH•tiks) are drugs that slow down the nervous system. Some narcotics are very useful for treating pain. Morphine (MOR•feen) and codeine (KOH•deen) are two such narcotics. These drugs are both made from the opium poppy plant. These drugs must be prescribed by a doctor. They can slow down the heart and breathing and can be addictive. They are very dangerous if a person takes them with alcohol.

Heroin (HEHR•uh•wuhn) is an illegal narcotic made from morphine. It can cause mental problems, infections, and breathing problems. Heroin is highly addictive. People who try to quit using heroin may have pain, upset stomachs, and movements they can't control. They may need medicine to help them handle the withdrawal symptoms.

**Analyze Concepts**

Answer each question in complete sentences.

20. A person who is addicted to drugs decides to get help. What are the steps in drug recovery that the person will go through?

22. How do advertisements try to influence the choices you make about alcohol use? How can you protect yourself from those influences?
23. Why is it important to know about the toxins in tobacco smoke?

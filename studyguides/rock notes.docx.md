**Rocks**\-natural solids made of one or more minerals, classified by how they form

**Igneous Rocks** \= form when molten material (lava or magma) cool and harden.

	\-magma – melted rock below Earth’s surface

	\-lava – melted rock above Earth’s surface

1\. intrusive (under Earth’s surface) \= when magma cools slowly and creates large crystals

	\-examples are granite and gabbro

2\. extrusive (above Earth’s surface) \= when lava cools quickly and creates small crystals or no crystals

	\-examples are basalt (small), obsidian (no crystals/glass)

\-some rocks cool with gas bubbles in them such as pumice and scoria

\-rock has high silica, it’s light in color	\-rock has low silica, it’s dark in color

\*samples \= obsidian, granite, basalt, gabbro, pumice, scoria

**Sedimentary Rocks** \= form when rock is weathered/eroded into sediment, the sediments get compacted and cemented together into rock.

\-weathering is breaking rock into smaller pieces (sediment)

\-erosion is moving the sediment

\-deposition is laying sediment in a new location (settles out of water or wind)

1\. clastic \= sediments compacted and cemented together

	\- fine-grained (small) like sandstone(sand) and shale(clay)

	\- coarse-grained (large) like conglomerate(pebbles)

2\. chemical \= mineral-rich water evaporates, leaving minerals behind as a rock

	\- stalactites – form on ceiling of caverns, hang down (limestone, halite)

	\- stalagmites – form on ground of caverns, build up (limestone, halite)

3\.organic \= remains of living things get compacted and cemented together

	\- plant remains like coal		\- shell remains like coquina

	\- fossils in limestone

\*samples \= coal, sandstone, shale, conglomerate, limestone, coquina

**Metamorphic Rocks** \= form when rock undergoes extreme heat and pressure

	\-commonly form under mountains, near volcanoes and under the ocean floor

	\- banded/foliated \= minerals band together and stripe the rock

	\- not banded/nonfoliated \= minerals are not in stripes

	\-schist has foliation, formed from sandstone	\-quartzite formed from sandstone

	\-gneiss has foliation, formed from granite	\-marble formed from limestone

	\-slate has foliation, formed from shale

\*samples \= gneiss, schist, slate, marble, quartzite, amphibolite

				**How rocks are used\!**

\*granite\&marble – countertop, floor, tombstone, building, monument, fireplace

\*sandstone\&limestone\&shale\&slate – garden wall/path, landscape, fireplace/firepit

\*rock salt – road\&walkways in winter

\*coal – fossil fuel(heat\&electricity)

\*quartzite – countertop

				**The Rock Cycle**

Rock Cycle \= continuous process in which one type of rock changes into another type of rock.

\- melted, cools and hardens \=\> Igneous Rock

\- weathered, eroded, deposited, compacted and cemented \=\> Sedimentary Rock

\- undergoes extreme heat and pressure \=\> Metamorphic Rock
